Filling out surverys and online feedback forms can be tedious and boring.

Say hello to Bluey!

The Messenger bot that facilitates surveys and feedback forms that are fun and easy.

Introduction

For our final week at Enspiral Dev Academy, each student was reqchatbotred to pitch an idea for final group projects, each team had six days to complete the project.

Technology

bluey used many different technologies. We used Express to run a server and Knex to assist with our SQL calls to our PostgreSQL database. We used handlebars for html templating, SASS for css, Jquery for DOM manipulation and nightwatch, selenium and OAuth for unit testing and authorisation. We deployed the final app to Heroku.

Our approach and process

We started with planning, developed user stories and figured out what technologies were best. We then moved to bchatbotlding wireframes for the site. Creating wireframes, user stories, and sitemaps from the start were important because it ensured that every team member was on the same page which made delegating roles and tasks straightforward.

We used InDesign to produce the wireframes and site maps which were bases for my chatbot design. Since we had less than a week to complete the app, we skipped the prototype stage and decided to move straight into development.

(Wireframes done with indesign)

Welcome to bluey

At midnight on the final day, there was a 'code freeze', which meant no more coding or improvements to the app. After 6 days of intense development and design, we had achieved our MVP and felt we had an app that we could proudly present.

There were still many areas that still needed work such as adding additional features and refactoring code. I would have liked to have done more iterations and improvements on the chatbot design, the app was not yet fully responsive and the CSS needed cleaning up. Everyone in the team had an amazing experience.
